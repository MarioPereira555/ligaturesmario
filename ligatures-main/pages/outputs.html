Outputs

Artifacts and outputs of the Ligatures SIG are of both digital and analog nature. This includes the creation and distribution of digital and physical typefaces, which are developed as part of the group's research and projects.

The latest and most important publication is the master thesis on "Chromatic Typography" from Tiago Rego. It has won the award of best poster in IJUP 2024. And has been accepted for presentation and publication in the 14th edition of the Typography Meeting.

Research Outputs

In addition to type design, the SIG actively produces research papers and technical documentation. These documents might take the form of white papers that delve into specific research findings, interviews that capture the insights of influential figures in design, or documented analyses of case studies related to typography, visual language, and design's cultural impact.

The group also seeks to bridge the gap between practice and theory by publishing resources that reflect on experimental and speculative design practices. These publications aim to foster interdisciplinary dialogues and encourage reflections on design's role in shaping cultural narratives.

All these outputs are made accessible to the broader public, aiming to inspire new researchers, students, and practitioners to explore the multifaceted nature of visual communication. This commitment to sharing knowledge not only helps preserve cultural and design legacies but also pushes the boundaries of what design can achieve in addressing contemporary challenges.
